Embark on a culinary journey to the vibrant streets of Morocco with this gluten-free twist on a classic favorite: Mini Zaatar and Olive Harsha. Harsha, a beloved Moroccan flatbread, is typically enjoyed as a breakfast staple or a comforting snack. My gluten-free adaptation maintains the authentic crispy exterior and soft, slightly sweet interior that harsha is known for. The addition of zaatar, an aromatic Middle Eastern spice blend, and briny black olives elevates this dish to new heights, creating a harmonious fusion of flavors that dance on your palate.

Picture
Ingredients for 8 mini harshas:

- 1 1/2 cups finely milled corn flour (not the coarse kind)
- 1 teaspoon baking powder
- 2 tablespoons sugar of choice (or honey)
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 50 grams (about 3 1/2 tablespoons) unsalted butter, melted
- 1/2 - 3/4 cup warm milk of choice (adjust as needed)

For the toppings:
- 1 tablespoon zaatar spice mix
- 1/2 cup pitted black olives, roughly chopped

Method:
1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the corn flour, baking powder, sugar (if using), and salt. Whisk together to ensure even distribution of the dry ingredients.

2. Pour the melted butter over the dry ingredients. Using your fingers or a fork, work the butter into the flour mixture until you achieve a crumbly texture, similar to wet sand.

3. Gradually add the warm milk, starting with 1/2 cup. Mix with a wooden spoon or your hands, adding more milk as needed. The goal is to create a dough that's moist enough to form patties but not overly wet. It should hold together when pressed.

4. Once you've reached the right consistency, add the zaatar spice mix and chopped olives to the dough. Gently fold them in, ensuring even distribution throughout the mixture.

5. Divide the dough into 8 equal portions. With slightly damp hands, shape each portion into a small patty, about 2-3 inches in diameter and 1/2 inch thick.

6. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium-low heat. You don't need to add any oil, as the butter in the dough will prevent sticking.

7. Place the harsha patties onto the heated surface, leaving some space between each one. Cook for 5-7 minutes on each side, or until they develop a golden-brown crust and are cooked through.

8. Carefully flip the harshas and cook for another 5-7 minutes on the other side. They should be crispy and browned on both sides when done.

9. Remove from the heat and let them cool slightly on a wire rack to maintain their crispiness.

- For longer storage, keep them in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.
- To freeze, place cooled harshas in a freezer-safe container, separating layers with parchment paper, and freeze for up to 1 month. Thaw at room temperature and reheat before serving.

The Science Behind the Ingredients

1. Brown Rice Flour: This gluten-free flour provides structure and a slightly nutty flavor. It's higher in protein compared to white rice flour, contributing to a better texture.
2. Tapioca Starch: Acts as a binder and helps create a chewy texture. It also aids in browning, giving the cookies a lovely golden color.
3. Corn Starch: Adds tenderness to the cookies and helps with moisture retention.
4. Corn Flour: Provides a subtle sweetness and helps create a crisp exterior.
5. Xanthan Gum: This is crucial in gluten-free baking as it mimics the elasticity and stickiness of gluten, helping to bind the ingredients together and prevent crumbling.
6. Baking Soda and Baking Powder: These leavening agents work together to provide the perfect rise. Baking soda reacts with the acidic ingredients (brown sugar and yogurt) for immediate leavening, while baking powder provides a secondary rise during baking.
7. Browned Butter: This is a key flavor enhancer. The process of browning butter creates nutty, caramel-like notes that add depth and richness to the cookies.
8. Sugars: The combination of dark brown and light brown sugars provides moisture, chewiness, and a complex sweetness. The molasses in brown sugar also reacts with the baking soda, aiding in leavening.
9. Greek Yogurt: This replaces eggs in the recipe. It adds moisture, helps with binding, and its acidity works with the baking soda for leavening. It also contributes to a tender crumb.
10. Vanilla Extract: Enhances all the other flavors in the cookie.
11. Chocolate: Provides bursts of flavor and textural contrast. Using a mix of sizes creates varied chocolate experiences in each bite.

Method

1. Brown the Butter:
   - Place the butter in a light-colored saucepan over medium heat.
   - Stir constantly as the butter melts, foams, and then begins to sputter.
   - Keep stirring until you see golden-brown specks forming at the bottom of the pan and the butter smells nutty.
   - Immediately remove from heat and transfer to a heat-safe bowl to cool.
   - Why brown the butter? This process creates complex flavor compounds that give the cookies a rich, nutty taste that can't be achieved with regular melted butter.

2. Prepare the Dry Ingredients:
   - In a medium bowl, whisk together the brown rice flour, tapioca starch, corn starch, corn flour, xanthan gum, salt, baking soda, and baking powder.
   - Mixing these ingredients separately ensures even distribution of the leavening agents and xanthan gum.

3. Mix the Wet Ingredients:
   - Once the browned butter has cooled to room temperature, add it to a large mixing bowl.
   - Add both types of sugar to the butter and mix until well combined.
   - Stir in the Greek yogurt and vanilla extract until the mixture is smooth.

4. Combine Wet and Dry:
   - Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined.
   - Be careful not to overmix, as this can lead to tough cookies.

5. Add Chocolate:
   - Fold in your chosen chocolate chips or chunks.

6. Chill the Dough:
   - Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate overnight, or for at least 4 hours.
   - This resting period allows the flours to fully hydrate and the flavors to meld. It also firms up the dough, preventing excessive spreading during baking.

7. Prepare for Baking:
   - Preheat your oven to 350Â°F (180Â°C).
   - Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

8. Shape the Cookies:
   - Remove the dough from the refrigerator.
   - Divide the dough into 8 equal portions, each weighing approximately 90-100g.
   - Roll each portion into a ball and place on the prepared baking sheet, leaving ample space between each cookie for spreading.

9. Bake:
   - Bake for 20-22 minutes, until the edges are visibly browned but the centers still look soft.
   - For crispier cookies, bake for an additional 3-5 minutes.

10. Cool:
    - Remove from the oven and let the cookies cool completely on the baking sheet.
    - The cookies will be very soft when first out of the oven but will firm up as they cool.
