Its texture is soft and yielding, with a gentle chew that begs for another bite. A hint of sweetness dances on the tongue, a subtle counterpoint to the savory dishes it often accompanies.

Moroccan families often tear pieces of Mkhamer to scoop up the rich 'marqa' or sauce of their tagines, that form the cornerstone of their meals. The soft bread soaks up the vibrant flavors, creating a delicious and satisfying bite in every mouthful. Beyond its functional role, Mkhamer also holds a social significance. Sharing a meal of Mkhamer with loved ones is a gesture of warmth and hospitality. It's a bread that brings people together, fostering conversation and laughter around the table.

This recipe offers a taste of Morocco in your own kitchen. Whether enjoyed hot off the pan or used as a base for flavorful fillings, this gluten-free recipe ensures that everyone can savor a piece of Moroccan tradition.

What Makes Mkhamer Special?

Unlike regular pita bread, Mkhamer offers a delightful textural experience. Imagine a thicker, fluffier flatbread with a satisfying chew. While it lacks the pronounced puff of a pita, Mkhamer makes up for it in pure comfort food appeal. A touch of sweetness lingers in the background, perfectly complementing savory fillings like cheese or spiced meats. This recipe lets you choose: enjoy them plain for a satisfying snack or transform them into flavor-packed pockets with the optional cheese filling. Mkhamer is a versatile bread perfect for a quick lunch, a light dinner accompaniment, or even a fun appetizer.

Ingredients (makes 6 Mkhamer):

Dry Ingredients:
120 g Rice Flour
20 g Oat Flour (or Sorghum Flour)
80 g Tapioca Starch (or Cornstarch/Potato Starch)
3 g Instant Yeast (or Active Dry Yeast, see note)
9 g Psyllium Husk Powder
5 g Salt
15 g Sugar
Wet Ingredients:
250-270 g Warm Water
2-3 tbsp Olive Oil
100 g Shredded Mozzarella Cheese (Optional)

Note: If using active dry yeast, you'll need to activate it before using it in the recipe. See the activation instructions below.
Method:
Activate Dry Yeast (Optional):
In a small bowl, combine 1 teaspoon of sugar with a little bit of your warm water. Sprinkle the yeast over the water and let it sit for 5-10 minutes. The mixture should become foamy, indicating the yeast is active. Proceed with the recipe as instructed, adding the activated yeast mixture along with the other wet ingredients.
Combine Dry Ingredients:
In a large bowl, whisk together the rice flour, oat flour (or sorghum flour), tapioca starch, instant yeast (or activated dry yeast), psyllium husk powder, and salt.
Add Wet Ingredients:
Gradually add the warm water to the dry ingredients, mixing with a dough whisk or a spoon until a shaggy dough forms.
Knead and Rest:
Once all the water is incorporated, knead for 5-7 minutes. The dough will be slightly sticky but should come together and form a smooth ball. Add half of the olive oil to the dough and knead it in until fully incorporated.
Rise:
Lightly coat a clean bowl with the remaining olive oil. Place the dough ball in the oiled bowl, turning it to coat all sides. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let it rise in a warm place for 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until doubled in size.
Divide and Shape:
Divide the risen dough into 6 equal portions. Roll each portion into a ball and cover them with a damp cloth to prevent drying.
Heat the Pan:
Place a cast iron skillet or a heavy-bottomed pan over medium heat. There's no need to add any oil to the pan.
Flatten and Fill (Optional): (See note below)
On a lightly floured surface, use a rolling pin to roll out one dough ball into your desired thickness. If using cheese, sprinkle a handful of shredded mozzarella in the center of the dough circle. Note: If not using cheese, simply roll out the dough ball and proceed to step 9.
Folding and Sealing: Carefully lift the edges of the dough circle and bring them together over the filling (if using), pinching the seams to seal. Shape the sealed dough ball back into ball then flatten again into your desired thickness with the rolling pin.
Cook the Mkhamer: Carefully transfer the Mkhamer to the preheated pan. Cook for 4-5 minutes per side over medium-low heat, or until golden brown spots appear on the bottom.
Repeat and Enjoy: Cook the remaining Mkhamer following the same steps. These flatbreads will puff up slightly when flipped, but not as much as regular pita bread.‚Äã

‚ÄãThese fluffy Mkhamer are perfect on their own, enjoyed warm from the pan. They make a delightful breakfast option with melted butter and drizzled with a touch of honey or maple syrup. For a more substantial meal, use them as a base for sandwiches filled with roasted vegetables, savory meats, or falafel. Their versatility extends beyond just handheld meals; they're also fantastic for soaking up the delicious juices of a Moroccan tagine.

Tips & Storage:
Dough Consistency: The dough will be slightly sticky, but it shouldn't stick excessively to your hands. If the dough feels too dry, add a tablespoon of water at a time until it reaches the desired consistency.
Cooking Temperature: Maintain medium-low heat when cooking the Mkhamer. This allows them to cook through evenly without burning the outside.
Leftovers: Store leftover Mkhamer in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days. You can refresh them by warming them in a skillet or toaster oven.

How to Cook Perfect Basmati Rice:

Basmati rice, known for its long, slender grains and fragrant aroma, perfectly complements the creamy butter chicken. Here's how to cook it:

Ingredients:
1 cup Basmati Rice
1 Water
1/2 tsp Salt

Method:
